Research Question
-------------------------------

1. Clear and Specific
=======================
- Is the research question clearly stated?
- Is it specific and focused on a particular issue or problem?

2. Researchable
================
- Can the question be answered through empirical data collection and analysis?
- Is it feasible to address within the constraints of time, resources, and expertise?

3. Relevant
===========
- Does the question address a significant issue in the field?
- Is it relevant to current trends and gaps in the literature?

4. Originality
============
- Is the question novel or does it offer a new perspective on an existing issue?
- Does it contribute to advancing knowledge in the field?

5. Complexity and Scope
================
- Is the question appropriately complex, avoiding being too broad or too narrow?
- Can it be explored in depth within the given scope of the project?

6. Alignment with Objectives
=====================
- Does the research question align with the objectives and goals of the study?
- Does it guide the research design and methodology effectively?

Literature Review
----------------

1. Comprehensive Coverage
========================
- Have you covered a wide range of sources relevant to your research question?
- Does the review include seminal works, recent studies, and key publications in the field?

2. Critical Analysis
=====================
- Have you critically evaluated the strengths and weaknesses of existing research?
- Are different perspectives and methodologies in the literature discussed?

3. Synthesis of Findings
=====================
- Have you synthesized findings from various studies to identify patterns, trends, and gaps?
- Do you draw connections between different pieces of research to build a cohesive narrative?

4. Contextualization
=================
- Does the review place your research question within the context of existing knowledge?
- Have you highlighted how your study will address gaps or build on prior research?

5. Current and Relevant Sources
====================
- Are the sources up-to-date and relevant to the current state of research?
- Have you included recent publications to ensure the review reflects the latest developments?

6. Methodological Rigor
=====================
- Have you assessed the methodological quality of the studies reviewed?
- Are the limitations and biases of previous research discussed?

7. Theoretical Framework
===================
- Does the literature review include relevant theories that underpin your research?
- Have you discussed how these theories relate to your research question and objectives?

8. Organization and Clarity
====================
- Is the literature review well-organized, with clear subheadings and logical flow?
- Are your arguments and discussions clearly articulated and easy to follow?

9. Citation and Referencing
======================
- Have you accurately cited all sources according to the required citation style?
- Is the reference list complete and formatted correctly?

10. Identification of Research Gap
====================
- Have you clearly identified a research gap that your study aims to address?
- Is the significance of this gap well-explained and justified?

If your research Project Follows this Checklist, then you have a project that is ready to move onto the methodology stage! If not, you may want to reconsider your project and redefine the areas that it lacks.
